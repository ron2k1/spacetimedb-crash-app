# Meeting Notes

This skill takes the raw notes you scribbled during a meeting and turns them into something you can actually share. You paste in whatever you have, even if it is just bullet points, half sentences, and names, and it gives you back a tidy summary and a clear list of next steps.

It follows a simple set of steps:

- Read through all of your notes and figure out what the meeting was really about.
- Write a short summary at the top, usually three to five sentences, so anyone can catch up in under a minute.
- Pull out every action item and list it as its own line, with the person responsible and a due date when one was mentioned.
- Flag any open questions or decisions that were left unresolved, so nothing quietly falls through the cracks.

The goal is to save you the boring cleanup work. Hand it your mess, get back a summary you can send to the team and a checklist you can act on. If a name or deadline is unclear, it will say so instead of guessing.
