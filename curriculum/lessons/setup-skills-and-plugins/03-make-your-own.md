# Make your own

Installing skills is handy, but the real power comes from making your own. For that you use the Skill Creator dashboard. This is where you describe a job in plain words and Crash turns it into a working skill, no coding required.

The flow is simple. First you describe your goal, like "take my class notes and turn them into a one-page study sheet." Next, Crash shows you a plan, a short outline of the steps the skill will follow. You read the plan and confirm it, or you tweak the wording until it matches what you actually want. Only after you confirm does Crash build the skill.

When it is done, you have a runnable skill saved in your working folder, just like the ones you installed from the marketplace. You can run it right away on real material, and you can run it again next week on something new. If the result is not quite right, you can adjust the skill and run it again.

That is the whole loop. Browse the marketplace when something already exists, and open the Skill Creator when you want something built around your own needs. From here, the rest is up to you. Crash sets up the base, and you take it wherever your work goes.
